# Calibration — magazine opener

**Date:** 2026-04-20
**Brief ask (§Your first three moves #3):** Write one paragraph of prose — the opening of a magazine article about a user of UKTI. Two hundred words, your voice.
**Rules followed:** no banned words, no adverbs of intensity, period-terminated fragments in the stylist's quoted register, locked copy echoed once ("Not the trench. Not the dress. Not the one you wore on Thursday."), third-person journalist voice.
**Count:** 194 words.

---

Mira is thirty-four, a translator, and she keeps her phone on the bathroom counter while she brushes her teeth. Tuesday, seven-twelve in the morning. The apartment is still in the grey part of the day. She opens the app and holds the phone up — just long enough for a still photograph to happen — and then she puts it back down. Camel knit. Black denim. Brown loafers. *Soft authority*, in italic. She reads the line twice. Not because she needs to, but because it's the kind of sentence you read twice. She dresses. It takes four minutes. She does not stand in front of the closet. She does not try the trench and then the dress and then the one she wore on Thursday. The knit is the one she bought in a shop in Lisbon, two autumns ago, and never knew what to do with. The stylist knew. She walks out of the building at seven-forty, notebook under her arm, a coffee she did not pay too much for, and the small calm of a woman who has been seen before the day started looking at her. She will come back tomorrow.
